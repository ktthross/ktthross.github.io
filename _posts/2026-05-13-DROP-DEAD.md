As of this writing, the new album cycle for Olivia Rodrigo is in full swing -- the tour is even announced!  I felt compelled to put something out into the world before the album drops!  Part fun, part passionate fan, and part hubris, this is my attempt to read the text of this cycle and make a prediction. I wanted to share a couple of things I really liked about the first single "<span style="color: #ff69b4;">Drop Dead</span>".  The interviews and rumors are that <span style="color: #ff69b4;">Drop Dead</span> will be the happiest song on the album, which from the title does not bode well for our girl.

<span style="color: #ff69b4;">Drop Dead</span> is the first single and the first song on the album.  This is the beginning of our narrative and Olivia is setting the stage for what is to come. It is safe to assume that this means the album will be much more narrative than her previous works. Individual songs off of SOUR or GUTS are narrative in that they tell stories about Olivia's internal state, but they are only connected insofar as they were written within the same general period of her life and chronicle themes from that time. The placement of <span style="color: #ff69b4;">Drop Dead</span> at the beginning of **You Seem Pretty Sad for a Girl So in Love** suggests that this album will be different in that each song will be connected by a large narrative thread that appears in each song and that each song will be arranged in the order that the events occurred.  At the least, even if the songs do not follow a strictly linear narrative, as far as the album cycle is concerned, Olivia is letting us know that this is the beginning.

So where do we find Olivia at the beginning?  The imagery of the single is a big indicator! The cover shows Olivia, dressed in pink, blowing a large bubble with her bubble gum.  In the most literal sense, Olivia is showing us that her bubble is about to burst.  The single is capturing Olivia in the moment before she <span style="color: #ff69b4;">drops dead</span>.  This theme is reinforced by the cover of **You Seem Pretty Sad for a Girl So in Love**. Olivia is seen in a swing, at the apex of her trajectory, so high on the swing that she is upside down.  The image is of her suspended in this moment, a moment before she <span style="color: #ff69b4;">drops dead</span>. She is showing us that gravity has different plans.

At face value, the song is the story of that initial feeling and experience of having a strong connection to someone and being overwhelmed with the desire to spend time with them.  A wonderful time in any relationship and worthy of a catchy pop song!  A closer examination reveals that <span style="color: #ff69b4;">Drop Dead</span> has another layer, however. Below the excitement, Olivia is telling us something more autobiographical and factual through the clever use of "<span style="color: #ff69b4;">drop dead</span>".

Let's look at the lyrics that give the song its name.
<blockquote>
The most alive I've ever been
<br>
But kiss me and I might <span style="color: #ff69b4;">drop dead</span>
</blockquote>

<span style="color: #ff69b4;">Drop dead</span> can take on multiple meanings. The first meaning is consistent with the song as a love song, <span style="color: #ff69b4;">drop dead</span> as an expression of extreme admiration or something being impressive. "The view from this house is so gorgeous I could just <span style="color: #ff69b4;">drop dead</span>". Olivia is effectively expressing barely contained joy at finding someone so amazing that, if they reciprocate her feelings, she might not survive it.  Through this interpretation we understand that Olivia feels extreme admiration for this person!

The second meaning is darker and more literal (but still figurative -- hurray contronyms).  Olivia is telling us quite literally, if she receives this kiss she will "<span style="color: #ff69b4;">drop dead</span>".  She will no longer be at the apex of her swing and she is not coming down in a controlled manner, she is dropping hard.  This is the event that catalyzes all the subsequent events and kicks off the narrative that will unfold in **You Seem Pretty Sad for a Girl So in Love**.

Olivia has really given us a fantastic single to start off the album cycle.  She has perfectly set the stage for what is to unfold. She shows us the excitement of a new relationship just bursting with anticipation and potential. Simultaneously, she uses that exact same song to show us that this is the start to a dark journey. She is about to fall and fall hard.

I am really looking forward to the full album and I hope you are too!
