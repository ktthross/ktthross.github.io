`You Look Pretty Sad For A Girl So In Love` is finally here. I have listened to it a few times and want to start collecting thoughts on each track. This is in no particular order and may just be observations and ideas at this point. A notepad for myself and yourself if you care to read. Hopefully I can synthesize this into a more concrete analysis with a thesis but every journey starts with a single step.

First impressions: the album is much more narrative and has a direct arc by which you can follow the events. GUTS and SOUR were more like collections of songs related by circumstance, but listening to them added context. The track order on her first two albums is for flow and tone. YLPSFAGSOIL, with maybe one exception, is enhanced by listening to the tracks in order and I would argue in some cases is necessary. There are songs which really should be a single track but due to constraints of the medium and industry you end up breaking them up.

The sound is very cohesive across the album.

It is revealing and Olivia is showing her own personal challenges with a lot of candor and honesty. Listening to it really made me think of `scared of my Guitar` in which she knows that she may be able to hide from others but she cannot hide in her music.

There are a few thematic elements which I haven't fully traced yet but will just drop here: medicine/cure, threads/unraveled/clothing/fabric, honeybee/sweetness.

Songs that immediately stood out to me: `Stupid Song`, `Maggots for Brains`, `U + Me = <3`, `What's Wrong with Me` and `Cigarette Smoke`.
